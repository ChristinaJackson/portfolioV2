Grandma's Cake Store

The Why:

The current image is of the project after I did some redesign work, original version can be seen in the above links. I coordinated the original version of this project because I wanted to gain experience developing collaboratively. I reached out to someone who I thought would be willing to participate and together we decided that we would build a mock store front page. I built the image grid, developed the high contrast mode for color accessibility, applied flex-box layout features to the header and footer as well as designing the footer. My partner implemented and adapted code for the cart from a tutorial and from there I added the filter on the products page as well as the feature to click out of cart area to close cart.

Tech used:

HTML/CSS, Javascript, Collaboration tools: Slack, Trello, Zoom

Challenges and things to improve:

The biggest challenge here was also the most fun part of this project. My partner on this project lives in a time zone 10 hours ahead of mine. One of us was working on the project at all times, which made it go quickly but coordination of tasks was difficult at first. We then used trello to track tasks that needed to be done and grabbed what we wanted to work on that day from the board.

Healing Waters Chiropractic

  • Live

The Why:

The owner of Healing Waters Chiropractic wanted a professional website for her Chiropractic practice. She wanted to be able to make small changes to the site without having deal with editing HTML or CSS. I chose to use Webflow to meet the clients needs.

Tech/features used:

Webflow, HTML/CSS, grid, flexbox

Challenges and things to improve:

The challenge for this project was learning a platform I had not used before in order to meet client needs. Luckily, the Webflow documentation was easy to navigate and did not slow down the expected speed or progress of the project. For this site I also made sure that it met WCAG guidelines for accessibility. The one challenge here was the client was intent on the color selection so there are some contrast issues that exist. There are plans to add high contrast features to the site once the client is ready.

Water Crisis Project

  • Live

The Why:

This project was built as a result of being contacted by the Water Crisis Project(WCP). WCP is working to provide residents of Flint, MI with water filters so that they can filter their contaminated water. The client wanted a simple site that they could link on their social media that would inform people of their goals and direct donations to the Water Crisis Project gofundme account.

Tech/features used:

HTML/CSS, scrollsnap, progressive enhancement, hosted on Firebase

Challenges and things to improve:

Because the Water Crisis project is a non-profit, they wanted a cheap hosting option so more dollars would be able to go to the people of Flint. In order to fulfill this admirable goal I chose to have their site hosted on Firebase which provides free hosting for smaller projects.
